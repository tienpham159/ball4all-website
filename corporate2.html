Vision

In a world where life’s mundane tasks overshadow joyful hobbies, we believe that you deserve to reach the balance between them, only then you can find freedom within your passion.

Brand Values

Flexibility At Ball4All, we understand the importance of balancing life's various commitments with recreational activities. Our platform is designed to offer maximum flexibility, incorporating features such as scheduling, booking, and group chats. These tools facilitate effective communication and organization, allowing users to easily integrate football into their busy lives.

Belonging The sense of belonging is at the heart of Ball4All. We strive to provide a safe and inclusive environment where everyone feels welcome to enjoy the beautiful game of football regardless of their skill level. The pillars of our community are respect, camaraderie and the shared passion for the sport.

Ease of use We aim to make our app as straightforward as possible in order to ensure the fact that everyone would be able to use it, since we believe that anyone is welcome to play football.

Freedom Ball4All values the freedom to play football on one's own terms. Whether it's choosing when and where to play, selecting teammates, or expressing oneself on the field, we empower our users to embrace the spirit of freedom that comes with the sport. We celebrate individuality, creativity, and the joy of unrestricted play within our football community.

  • Color palette
    • #FF8B00 According to color psychology, orange is often associated with feelings of enthusiasm and excitement. Because orange is a high-energy color, many sports teams use orange in their uniforms, mascots, and branding. We use orange to appeal to the primarily young demographic of our customer segment and express the playful, energetic side of our brand personality.
    • #889600 The color green relates to balance, growth, and a sense of community. Through this color, we want to convey the team spirit often seen in football games and the flexibility that our brand offers.     • #323C13, #EDEAE4 Since our final products include a website and a mobile app, we chose dark green and beige instead of black and white to maintain the necessary contrast in colors but also heighten the website/app’s UX/UI, since black and white is reported to cause exhaustion if the user looks at the screen for an extended period of time.
  • Font choices
    The typeface If Kica in uppercase is utilized for our logo as well as headings for our campaign. The typeface design is horizontally wide with equal weight for each letter, which expresses energy, masculinity, and modernity. As a unique display typeface, If Kica reflects and differentiates the brand’s personality from other brands. However, paying attention to the UX/UI aspect, we chose Poppins as the primary typeface for content-heavy designs. Poppins’ letters are designed with clean lines/circles within a square-shaped container, which is youthful but also maintains readability when displayed on website/mobile app/social platforms. This typeface also has a variety of weights and styles that allow versatility in design.

    Testing Report

  • Testing overview

    Testing Goals: The objective of these tests is to evaluate the efficiency, usability, brand recognition and the overall format of our website which aims to provide an efficient, community-oriented and diverse platform for amateur football players to set up schedules, book pitches and connect with other players on and off the pitch. The primary goal is clear recognition of our brand’s purpose and a guarantee that the services we provide meet the needs of our target customers. We want to find rooms for improvement in terms of UI/UX, meaning what has worked well for our website and which feature/service needs enhancement.

    Testing Participants: For our testings, we have chosen three male participants between the age of 20-23 who are amateur football players. These participants are selected based on our target audience for the brand.

    Testing Setting: The testing of our brand was done both on laptop and mobile, with one test done on mobile format and two tests done on laptop format. The testings were all done on campus to optimize the internet connection. All participants were invited to do the tests separately with two of our interviewers to minimize external influences on the test results.

    Testing Protocol: Before filming/recording, each participant will be informed of an overview of our brand, the purpose of the testing and necessary instructions. For the next step, participants will be asked to test each features, at the same time giving their opinions and questions. Meanwhile, interviewers will record participants' interactions, acknowledging any difficulties, confusion, or positive experiences. The testing process consisted of the following steps: Setting up a digital Baller Passport, Booking a Pitch, Match with other players, Clicking on different navigation features.

    Testing Results: Participants identified both disadvantages and advantages of different website features/services.

  • Positive Aspects of UI/UX:

    Easy Navigation: Users found the layout and navigation of the website to be clear and easy, which was great for finding relevant information, especially for a website that offers services.

    Community-oriented design: Users greatly engaged with the services provided by the website, especially the matchmaking feature. Users stated that the feature was really interesting and helpful for socializing.

    Overall Branding and Design: Users acknowledge the cohesive branding throughout the website. The website shows a cohesive football/sports theme which quickly engages the users in a visually appealing and welcoming environment.

    Social Media and Contact Accessibility: Users appreciated the website footer that included social media links and contact information, making it quick and easy to access.

  • Negative Aspects of UI/UX:

    Confusing Wording: Several participants encountered issues when filling in the "Location" section.

    Error with Phone Number Entry: One participant experienced a significant issue when entering his phone number. The error message that appeared was misleading, suggesting that the participant needed to type in his email address instead.

    Ambiguous Page Functionality: Some participants had difficulties understanding the purpose and functionality of the matchmaking page. The page lacked clear instructions and context, leaving users unsure of how to use the feature or what benefits it offered. This ambiguity led to underutilization of the feature and a potential loss of engagement from users who could not see its value.

  • Improvements to be Implemented on the Final Website:

    Clarifying Wording: To eliminate confusion, we will replace the term "Location" with "Place of Residence." This more specific wording will help users understand exactly what information is required, whether it be their city, state, or full address. By providing clearer instructions, we aim to enhance the user experience and reduce errors during form completion.

    Consistent Navigation Throughout the Website: While most participants were able to navigate the website efficiently, we will implement a more cohesive navigation structure to ensure all pages are clearly linked. This will help users understand the purpose of each page. Additionally, we will add detailed instructions and a call to action on the matchmaking tab to guide users on how to use the feature effectively. This will improve clarity and engagement, making the feature more accessible and valuable to users.

    Fixing Non-Functional Features: As our app gains more customers, we will enhance the matchmaking feature by linking it to the selected player's baller passport. For instance, when users click on Kenneth Tran, they will be able to view his baller passport, which includes comprehensive details about his skills, experience, and stats. Furthermore, we will integrate a chat function, allowing users to communicate directly with players or support staff. These enhancements will not only improve functionality but also foster a more interactive and engaging user experience, encouraging users to make the most of the app's features.

    Context of campaign and promotional activities

    Our Brand

    At Ball4all, we are on a mission to help users create an easily accessible community amongst amateur football players. In doing research, we discovered that football is an extremely common and popular team sport in The Netherlands, therefore we decided to focus on football to obtain maximum engagement and strengthen a community that is already visible in The Netherlands. In a world where people often have incredibly busy schedules, we aim to provide an accommodating and flexible platform that allows users to still participate in their passion. Our campaign is not only about promoting a product, it is about allowing people to celebrate the joy of football.

    What is the Campaign About?

    The Ball4All campaign revolves around distributing content that builds excitement while introducing our brand features. Through our posts we aim to build a community in the football world, demonstrating how we plan to make their life easier while gaining their trust by incorporating real people’s opinions through street interviews. Through our campaign we aim to spark meaningful conversations and represent the individuals who make up the community of The Netherlands football scene.

    The Message

    The tagline for our campaign is “FWB. Football with Benefits. All the Benefits without Commitment”. Through the tagline our brand aims to demonstrate a company that provides features to help make a footballer's experience and life easier, allowing them to access benefits of the sport without having to commit to a club. Our message is consistently represented throughout our content and social media platforms, including the tagline in videos and our bio, as well as having posts that further explain how we plan to execute our vision.

    Promotional Activities Executed
    To bring our message to life and connect with our audience, we've executed a variety of promotional activities:
    • Instagram and TikTok Content: We've launched engaging campaigns on Instagram and TikTok to showcase our brand and its features. Through street interviews, brand feature descriptions, brand collaborations and company introduction posts, we have worked towards inviting our viewers to become part of the growing community.
    • Brand Collaborations: We have reached out to other brands to collaborate and share each other's content. Through this process we attempted to gain brand connections while continuously promoting and growing our brand.
    • Interactive Components and Trends: We've launched interactive components on our stories in an attempt to once again make viewers feel they are connected with the brand and have a voice. In addition, we have posted videos following trends such as street interviews as well as using trending audios in our video to gain traction.
    • Hashtags: In each of our posts we ensured to include at least 3-4 hashtags in the caption. These often consisted of tags that correlated with our post, as well as tags that are popular in the football community.

    The Objective - SMART

    (Specific) We want to grow brand awareness by creating different types of content on both Instagram and TikTok. Our content will feature street interviews where we go around the hotspots of Breda, interacting directly with our target audience. Moreover, in these interviews, we ask questions about their difficulties as an amateur football player, whilst also asking about the usability of our features. Therefore, by posting these kinds of videos, we predict that most of our target audience have faced the same problems, creating a sense of belonging. In addressing the difficulties of amateur football players and seeking feedback on our brand features, it will allow our team to create a relatable brand, while genuinely addressing our target audience's needs. Creating a foundation of trust when building a long-lasting community.

    (Measurable) Our goal would be to reach 3000 impressions on both platforms. Population of Breda (since we’re going to interact with it the most) 180.000. 6% of the Netherlands’ population play football, extrapolating and applying it to the population of Breda, we have 10.800. 50% are males and given the fact our target audience is based on male amateur football players between the ages of 18-24, 3000 impressions is predicted to be an achievable goal.

    (Achievable) By reaching our goal, we will attract more customers towards the website/app, building a long-lasting community in time.

    (Timely) We’d like to achieve this goal within a month.

    The Selected Channels/Platforms

    We chose Instagram and TikTok as our channels due to the fact that they are the most relevant social media platforms for our target audience, male amateur football players aged 18-23. We got to this conclusion based on interviews with our target audience, all of them mentioning both. However, most of them use TikTok for entertainment and Instagram for both news and entertainment. Therefore, we chose to use Instagram for more “serious” posts such as our feature showcase, and TikTok for more funny, light-hearted content such as “types of footballers”.

    In addition, Instagram and TikTok are both known for their engaging formats, providing an ideal platform for sharing content and gaining viewers. The platforms are powerful tools in building brand visibility and reaching a large audience, providing our brand the opportunity to introduce our company to a variety of potential customers. Through the various features that platforms provide, such as stories, reels, reposts and short TikTok our team is provided flexibility to share our content in various forms. Including call-to-action in our posts and utilizing engagement features on our stories, it encourages a relationship with our audience. The platforms allow our team to experiment and discover which format captures our audience's attention most.

    Therefore, we utilized the platform's key benefits by incorporating trending audios, hashtags and many additional engagement tactics, in an attempt to reach our goal of 3000 impressions. The platforms provide a sense of community as it often caters to viewers interests, allowing football players in the Netherlands to discover our curated content for the sport.

    Future Planning

    In reflecting on our marketing journey, it is evident that our campaign has undergone significant growth as over time we began to utilize more features on the platform to promote our content. However, it is evident that we did not reach our goal of 3000 impressions. If the project were to continue, we would be able to use the experience we have gained in the month of content creation to implement a strategy aimed at further enhancing engagement and solidifying our brand identity. The following recommendation represents a detailed outline of how we would strive for future success.

    1. Transition to a Business Account: The first recommendation and change our team would implement is making our social media accounts a business profile from the beginning. Transforming our platforms into business accounts would allow us to have access to in-depth analytics, representing the growth our company has shown over time and from the beginning. Having this tool would enable us to review engagement insights and reflect on what we can do for each post to improve, such as posting at a different time/day, using different hashtags, and discovering the type of content that our audience enjoys most.

    2. Strategic Hashtags: In using hashtags, we believe it has helped our brand to get engagement; therefore, in the future of our campaign, we would continue to utilize relevant and trending hashtags across both social media platforms. However, a change we would implement is doing further research on what hashtags are trending and incorporating a lot more hashtags than 4-5, in order to enhance the chance of viewers discovering our company.

    3. Establishing a Consistent Tone of Voice: In reflecting, we discovered that it is crucial to establish a consistent tone of voice that resonates with our target audience from the beginning. Initially, our team wanted to represent a more serious and cinematic approach to our content. However, after doing research, we quickly realized the type of content our target audience typically views, which is more laid back. In addition, we realized the importance of our tone, which was incorporating slang versus professional text, which resonates more with our audience. Therefore, in creating a distinct brand voice, it allows us to connect more with our audience and establish a stronger brand identity.

    4. Posting More: In reflecting on the journey of our campaign, it is evident that we should have begun posting content sooner and more frequently. On platforms such as TikTok, posting every day allows a greater chance to reach viewers and appear on users' pages. In addition, posting more frequently allows viewers to engage with content and develop a stronger understanding and relationship with the brand.

    Overall, we believe in future planning, by following these future recommendations, discovered through research and content analytics, we believe we can redirect our campaign to increase visibility and growth.

    Lean canvas modifications

    • Customer segments- We realized that there’s a significant difference in lifestyles between age 18-25 and 25-30. The academic, professional, and social commitments will differentiate age 18-25 from 25-30 regarding football playing habits, spending habits, and availability. As a result, we modify our customer segment to age group 18-23 for a more focused brand identity and services.
    • Problems- Based on the problem interviews, we were able to identify two main problems: Communication: The lack of a convenient and cohesive communicating platform prevents the players from finding available teammates, regulating matches, and looking for good facilities. Miscommunication causes a lot of disruptions in playing time which is already limited due to work and personal life. Schedule flexibility: The misalignment in availability makes it difficult for players to meet up and organize matches together.
    • Solutions: We came up with three brand features that potentially solve the identified problems: Availability input: The players are able to set up their schedules on the website to see which fields/players are available at a chosen time frame. Pitch booking: The feature introduces a list of available pitches for players to choose at a specific time frame. Digital baller passport: A digital passport for players to display their information and collect digital stamps given to them after each match. Match-making: The feature matches players with other players who are at a specific skill level and available at a chosen time frame. We expected these features to solve disruptions caused by the communication and flexibility issues as well as motivate players to play football.
    • Unfair advantage: During our problem interviews, we noticed that the majority of our interviewees expressed great satisfaction towards the sense of accomplishment that scoring goals provides them. As such, we came up with the idea of having a digital baller passport as one of the main brand features. The 2-page passport will show players’ information and availability and a blank space for dated digital stamps (our brand logo) acquired after finishing a match. The passport also allows other players to have basic knowledge of their teammates, making socializing and teaming up easier.
    • Cost structure- After our problem interviews, we realized that an app would be more practical for our target audience, so we pivoted towards it. However, we decided to keep the website as a corporate page, given the fact that it is easily accessible. The maintenance cost of an app usually varies based on how complex the app is. Given the fact that our app is straightforward and easy to use, a rough estimate would be around 230 euros per month. Moreover, our website is used mainly to showcase our features, serving as a portal towards the app mostly. Therefore, an estimated maintenance cost for the website would be 370 euros per year. For our marketing campaign, we will collaborate with university students that have (or study towards) a Marketing specialization, for instance BUas Creative Business students during their production house days.     • Our solution interviews focused on confirming the problems and collecting reviews on our product prototype to see if we should pivot or persevere with the current direction.

    • One interviewee expressed great frustration (laughing, talking with sarcasm) towards the inconvenience of having to contact people and synchronize schedules through Whatsapp.

    • The interviewees expressed enthusiasm about a platform where availability options are provided, because it helps them gather a team and organize matches more quickly and easily.
    • The interviewees find it unnecessary for a skill level input feature, because to them “winning is important but fun is the top priority”. It depends on each player’ purpose of playing football.
    • The interviewees looked forward to the feature where they can see the availability of the pitches which allows them to book in advance, because they’d prefer to have a familiar field to play regularly to having to switch fields every week.
    • One interviewee stated that it would be helpful to add players’ (preferred) positions to their profile.
    • One interviewee said that they really enjoyed the fact that you can see who you are playing with beforehand and not find out on the spot.
    • One interviewee said that we should include a second feature in which they can also invite friends to join their team.

    • The customers recognized the problems and found most product features helpful. We removed the skill level feature and decided to persevere with the rest.
